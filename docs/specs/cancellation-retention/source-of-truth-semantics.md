# Reorder: Cancellation & Retention Source-of-Truth and Responsibility Boundaries Spec

This document covers step `2.5.2` from `documentation/implementation_plan.md`.

Goal:
- define the source-of-truth split between `Subscriptions`, `Renewals`, `Dunning`, and `Cancellation & Retention`
- decide what state belongs to `Subscription`
- decide what state belongs to `CancellationCase`
- decide what belongs to `RetentionOfferEvent`
- decide whether one subscription may have more than one active cancellation case
- define how cancellation handling interacts with an active `DunningCase`
- define how cancellation handling affects future `RenewalCycle`

This specification builds on:
- `reorder/docs/specs/subscriptions/domain-model.md`
- `reorder/docs/specs/renewals/source-of-truth-semantics.md`
- `reorder/docs/specs/dunning/source-of-truth-semantics.md`
- `reorder/docs/specs/cancellation-retention/trigger-entry.md`

The direction follows Medusa patterns:
- each module should own one clear operational aggregate
- cross-domain coordination should happen through workflows and explicit process rules, not overlapping ownership
- aggregate state and history records should be separated when they serve different operational purposes
- read-model enrichment may cross modules, but the aggregate root and primary state machine must remain explicit

Implementation status:
- `Cancellation & Retention` is not implemented yet
- this document is the design-time source of truth for ownership and state boundaries of the future area

## 1. Core semantics

The recurring commerce flow has four separate operational concerns:

- ongoing customer subscription state
- execution state of one due renewal cycle
- recovery state of one failed collectible payment event
- operator-managed cancellation and retention process state

These concerns must not be merged.

### Subscription operational state

`Subscriptions` own the customerâ€™s ongoing recurring contract.

This includes:
- whether the subscription is `active`, `paused`, `cancelled`, or `past_due`
- the active product and variant
- the active cadence
- scheduling anchors such as `next_renewal_at`
- operational snapshots such as shipping, product, and pricing context
- pending but not yet applied subscription changes

### Renewal execution state

`Renewals` own one concrete billing execution unit.

This includes:
- when one renewal cycle is due
- whether that cycle is `scheduled`, `processing`, `succeeded`, or `failed`
- whether approval is required and what was decided
- which order was generated by that cycle
- what attempts were made to execute that cycle

### Dunning recovery state

`Dunning` owns payment recovery after a renewal failed with a payment-qualified failure.

This includes:
- whether a failed debt event is open for recovery
- how many recovery attempts were made
- when the next retry is due
- what the latest payment recovery error is
- whether the debt was recovered or closed unrecovered

### Cancellation and retention process state

`Cancellation & Retention` owns the offboarding and save-flow process once an operator starts cancellation handling.

This includes:
- whether cancellation handling is open, in progress, or terminally resolved
- what churn reason and category were recorded
- what recommendation or save direction was selected
- what retention offers were proposed, accepted, rejected, or applied
- what final process outcome was reached

## 2. Source of truth by concern

### Subscription source of truth

The source of truth for the customerâ€™s lifecycle and active operational subscription state remains `Subscription`.

The `subscription` record owns:
- lifecycle status
- active cadence
- active product and variant
- next renewal scheduling anchor
- operational snapshots
- pending but not yet applied subscription changes
- final lifecycle timestamps such as `paused_at`, `cancelled_at`, and `cancel_effective_at`

### Cancellation process source of truth

The source of truth for the cancellation and save-flow process should be `CancellationCase`.

The `cancellation_case` record should own:
- the process state machine for offboarding and retention handling
- churn-reason capture and reason category
- notes and operator-entered context
- recommended action or recommended save direction
- final outcome of the process
- audit summary such as who finalized the process and when

### Retention history source of truth

The source of truth for offer and decision history should be `RetentionOfferEvent`.

The `retention_offer_event` record should own:
- one concrete retention offer proposal
- the proposed payload for that offer
- the decision made on that offer
- who made the decision and when
- whether and when that offer was actually applied

This should be append-only process history.

### Renewal source of truth

The source of truth for renewal execution remains `RenewalCycle`.

`Cancellation & Retention` must not replace:
- `RenewalCycle.status`
- approval state
- generated order reference
- attempt history

### Dunning source of truth

The source of truth for payment-recovery state remains `DunningCase`.

`Cancellation & Retention` must not replace:
- dunning status
- retry schedule
- retry attempt history
- recovery closure state

## 3. What belongs to `Subscription`

`Subscription` should continue to own:
- lifecycle status such as `active`, `paused`, `cancelled`, `past_due`
- current active product and cadence
- current next renewal schedule
- shipping, pricing, product, and customer snapshots
- pending subscription updates
- final timestamps and fields reflecting lifecycle effects after the cancellation process materializes an outcome

`Subscription` should not own:
- the state machine of cancellation handling
- offer history for retention attempts
- the decision timeline of churn handling
- the current recommendation chosen inside the cancellation process

Why:
- these belong to the process, not to the lifecycle snapshot
- the subscription should remain the stable operational contract, not the event log of offboarding decisions

## 4. What belongs to `CancellationCase`

`CancellationCase` should be the aggregate root of the cancellation and retention journey.

It should own:
- whether cancellation handling is currently active
- the current case status
- the recorded churn reason and category
- notes and operator-entered context
- recommendation state
- final process outcome such as retained, paused, or canceled
- audit summary fields such as who finalized the case and when

It should not own:
- the subscriptionâ€™s active product or cadence
- payment recovery state
- renewal-cycle execution state
- the full append-only history of every offer decision as mutable aggregate fields

Why:
- `CancellationCase` is the process record, not the long-term subscription snapshot
- aggregate-level state should remain compact and queryable
- detailed offer history should live in a dedicated child record

## 5. What belongs to `RetentionOfferEvent`

`RetentionOfferEvent` should be the append-only history entity for concrete save actions and decisions.

It should own:
- `offer_type`
- `offer_payload`
- `decision_status`
- `decision_reason`
- `decided_at`
- `decided_by`
- `applied_at`
- `metadata`

It should not own:
- the current aggregate case state
- the final lifecycle state of the subscription
- the final outcome of the whole cancellation process

Why:
- this is analogous to:
  - `RenewalAttempt` under `RenewalCycle`
  - `DunningAttempt` under `DunningCase`
- detailed history should remain append-only
- aggregate state should stay on `CancellationCase`

## 6. Active-case uniqueness

One subscription should have at most one active `CancellationCase` at a time in MVP.

### Final decision

Recommended invariant:
- one subscription may have many historical cancellation cases over time
- one subscription may have only one active cancellation case at any given moment

Why this is preferred:
- operators should not manage two competing offboarding flows for one subscription
- Admin UX remains simpler and more consistent
- analytics and final-outcome reporting remain unambiguous
- this matches the same conservative operational rule already used by `Dunning`

### Meaning of active

Active means:
- the case is not terminally resolved
- the process can still accept retention decisions or move to final cancellation

Historical multiplicity is still allowed once prior cases are closed.

## 7. Relationship with active `DunningCase`

An active `DunningCase` should not automatically block opening a `CancellationCase`.

### Final decision

Recommended behavior:
- a subscription may have both an active `DunningCase` and an active `CancellationCase`
- each case remains the source of truth for its own process
- the conflict must be explicit in workflow rules and later Admin reads, not hidden by implicit ownership overlap

Why this is preferred:
- `Dunning` answers whether unpaid debt is under recovery
- `Cancellation & Retention` answers whether the operator is managing offboarding or save actions
- these are different questions and should remain separate operational threads

### What this does not mean

This does not mean:
- `CancellationCase` may close or mutate `DunningCase` implicitly at creation time
- `DunningCase` may decide cancellation outcome
- one process may silently overwrite the otherâ€™s state

Instead:
- coexistence is allowed
- later workflows must define explicit resolution behavior when a final cancellation or pause outcome should affect dunning recovery handling

## 8. Relationship with future `RenewalCycle`

`Cancellation & Retention` should not own `RenewalCycle`.

`Renewals` remain the owner of:
- queue records
- execution state
- attempt history
- renewal-level approval and generated order references

### Final decision

Cancellation handling should influence future renewals indirectly through `Subscription` state and explicit workflow materialization, not by taking ownership of renewal cycles.

This means:
- opening a cancellation case does not itself replace or delete renewal ownership
- final process outcomes such as `paused` or `canceled` must later materialize into subscription lifecycle state
- renewals should then respect the resulting subscription state according to renewal eligibility rules

### Why this is preferred

Why this is preferred:
- `RenewalCycle` remains one billing-execution unit
- `CancellationCase` remains one operator-managed offboarding unit
- the responsibilities stay separate and easier to reason about
- future workflow coordination can stay explicit and testable

## 9. State ownership by domain

The domains should own state in the following way.

### 9.1 State owned by `Subscription`

`Subscription` owns:
- current lifecycle state
- current active cadence and product
- current renewal eligibility context through lifecycle and scheduling fields
- final lifecycle timestamps after a pause or cancellation outcome is materialized

`Subscription` does not own:
- the cancellation-handling process state
- retention-offer history
- dunning retry state
- renewal-attempt history

### 9.2 State owned by `CancellationCase`

`CancellationCase` owns:
- the cancellation-handling status machine
- current offboarding decision state
- churn classification and operator-entered case context
- final process outcome

`CancellationCase` does not own:
- the subscriptionâ€™s active cadence
- the dunning retry lifecycle
- the renewal cycleâ€™s execution state
- append-only offer history details that should belong to `RetentionOfferEvent`

### 9.3 State owned by `RetentionOfferEvent`

`RetentionOfferEvent` owns:
- one concrete offer or save-action history entry
- decision and application audit for that offer

`RetentionOfferEvent` does not own:
- the aggregate state of the case
- the final lifecycle state of the subscription

### 9.4 State owned by `RenewalCycle`

`RenewalCycle` owns:
- one due billing event
- execution and approval state for that event
- generated order outcome
- attempt history through `RenewalAttempt`

`RenewalCycle` does not own:
- cancellation-process state
- retention-offer decision history
- dunning retry state

### 9.5 State owned by `DunningCase`

`DunningCase` owns:
- the payment-recovery lifecycle of one failed collectible debt event
- retry schedule and retry attempts
- recovery closure outcome

`DunningCase` does not own:
- cancellation-process state
- churn classification and retention recommendation state
- final subscription cancellation decision

## 10. Summary decision

The source-of-truth boundary for MVP is:

- `Subscription` remains the source of truth for lifecycle and active operational subscription state
- `CancellationCase` becomes the source of truth for cancellation and retention process state
- `RetentionOfferEvent` becomes the append-only history of retention offers and decisions
- one subscription may have only one active `CancellationCase`
- an active `DunningCase` does not block an active `CancellationCase`, but both remain separate operational aggregates
- future `RenewalCycle` records remain owned by `Renewals`, while cancellation affects them indirectly through final materialized subscription state

This gives the future `Cancellation & Retention` area a clear boundary:
- one aggregate for process state
- one child history record for retention decisions
- explicit separation from lifecycle, renewal, and payment-recovery ownership
